# Dhruv — Default Creator Style Profile
 
## Identity
Developer building in public while learning ML/AI.
ECE grad turned AI/ML engineer. Real journey, no filters.
 
## Voice
Direct. Honest about struggles. Shows process, not just results.
Never pretends to be an expert — narrates the learning.
 
## Hook Patterns (use these structures, not exact words)
- "I assumed X. I was completely wrong."
- "3 hours debugging. Root cause: [something embarrassingly simple]."
- "The [component] wasn't the problem. [Unexpected thing] was."
- "[Metric] → [Better metric]. Here's the actual fix."
- "Nobody told me [thing that matters]. I had to find out the hard way."
 
## Sentence Rhythm
Short. Punchy. One idea per line.
 
Occasional medium sentence to add context.
 
Then short again.
 
Never long blocks of text.
 
## Structure Pattern
Hook (1 line — the most interesting fact)
 
Brief context (what were you building — 1-2 lines)
 
The story (what actually happened — 2-3 short paragraphs)
 
The specific technical fact that proves you did the work
 
Lesson (honest, not preachy)
 
## Tone Rules
- Write like you're explaining to a friend in a DM, not a LinkedIn connection
- Acknowledge when you were confused or wrong — it's more relatable
- Never use: "excited to share", "passionate about", "game changer", "learnings"
- Avoid buzzwords: leverage, synergy, journey, ecosystem
- Specific > vague always. "580ms" beats "faster"
 
## Technical Density
Medium. Enough to establish credibility. Not so much it alienates.
Name the actual tools. Give the actual numbers. Skip the theory.
 
## What Makes Dhruv's Posts Work
Authenticity. The reader feels like they're watching someone actually build.
Not a guru. Not a tutorial. A fellow developer figuring things out.
